Certificate Hall of Fame

Courses, specializations and badges, oh my!

No, it's not about showing off

The very existence of a certificate "Hall of Fame" (pretentious much?) might seem eccentric at best and narcissistic at worst. Who am I to declare my simple achievements as outstanding and worthy of induction into an elite group that doesn't exist except in my imagination? Rest assured, though, this has nothing to do with bragging rights. In fact, it serves some pretty practical purposes.

I believe that we can learn something new from pretty much anywhere. Big courses, small courses, edtech giants like Coursera or fledglings like LinkedIn Learning- it doesn't matter where it's from. But with all my achievements scattered across various platforms, it's harder to get a sense of how far I've come on my journey (and let's be honest, no one likes a LinkedIn page with an overflowing "Licenses and Certifications" section).

This space helps me to see all I've done at a glance, and not just a few recent certificates with the rest hidden away. And unlike on LinkedIn where less "important" courses waste space and create clutter, everyone has a place here. Whether a course spans a couple of months or a couple of hours, I gave each one its own space to shine. Because every minute spent learning is time spent chasing long-term goals, resisting distractions, and investing in myself. And on the days when nothing seems to go right and I'm enshrouded in darkness- every star, big and small, of my little galaxy will be there to light the way.
